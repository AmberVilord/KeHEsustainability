Idle-to-Impact: Three Proven Tactics

This strategy focuses on improving existing assets already in service instead of requiring fleet replacement or experimental technology.

Rather than proposing a single new technology, this proposal focuses on combining several proven operational improvements into one measurable, phased implementation strategy.

Fleet Idle-Reduction and Efficiency Retrofits

Idle-to-Impact starts with operational controls that reduce unnecessary fuel burn while preserving current dispatch and loading workflows.

  • idle reduction systems during loading, staging, and waiting periods
  • telematics-driven idle control policies and performance tracking
  • targeted efficiency retrofits for high-utilization tractors

Immediate fuel waste reduction without replacing vehicles.

SmartWay-Verified Aerodynamic Trailer Upgrades

High-mileage trailers on highway-heavy routes are prioritized for proven drag-reduction improvements that lower fuel use at cruising speeds.

  • deploy upgrades where highway usage is highest
  • prioritize routes with strongest payback potential
  • track fuel trend change before and after retrofit

Proven technology with measurable ROI and low operational disruption.

Selective Rail Integration for Long-Haul Corridors

Long-haul routes with predictable, high-volume demand can shift to intermodal rail while local truck delivery remains unchanged at destination hubs.

  • identify corridors where rail maintains service reliability
  • shift only stable lanes with repeatable freight volumes
  • retain trucks for final-mile flexibility and customer service

Rail handles distance; trucks maintain service continuity.

Unique Add-On Idea: Dynamic Platooning-as-a-Service (DPaaS)

In addition to immediate retrofit improvements, KeHE could pilot dynamic cross-fleet highway platooning partnerships that allow trucks to form temporary coordinated convoys, reducing aerodynamic drag and fuel consumption while requiring minimal new infrastructure.

This is not permanent platoons and not company-only fleets. It is a real-time matching approach where compatible carriers temporarily coordinate speed windows and dissolve automatically when routes diverge.

  • rare differentiator: cross-fleet dynamic matching, not just generic platooning claims
  • physics-backed: trailing trucks commonly show measurable fuel benefits in pilot programs
  • KeHE fit: predictable interstate corridors and scheduled windows already create match opportunities

Think: Waze-style convoy coordination for freight efficiency.
