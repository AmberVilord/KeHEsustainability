Circular Fleet Operations

Sustainability Doesn’t Stop When the Truck Stops

While fuel efficiency and routing improvements reduce emissions on the road, fleet maintenance and material recovery programs play an equally important role in building a responsible logistics system.

KeHE’s sustainability approach includes exploring circular maintenance practices that reduce waste, conserve resources, and lower long-term operational costs.

Section 1 — Closed-Loop Oil Recovery

Recycling Lubricants and Fluids

Heavy-duty trucks require large volumes of engine oil, transmission fluid, and hydraulic lubricants. Proper recovery and recycling programs allow these materials to be re-refined or reused in industrial processes, reducing environmental impact and limiting hazardous waste.

Why it matters

Re-refining used oil requires significantly less energy than producing lubricants from crude petroleum.

Recovered oil reduces both environmental risk and resource demand.

Section 2 — Remanufactured Component Programs

Extending the Life of Critical Parts

Fleet maintenance replaces major components such as starters, alternators, and engine assemblies. Certified remanufactured components reuse existing metal housings and structural parts, reducing the need for new material extraction and manufacturing.

Why it matters

Remanufactured components often require substantially fewer raw materials and energy compared to producing entirely new parts.

Reusing core components lowers lifecycle manufacturing impact.

Section 3 — Commercial Tire Retreading

Extending Tire Lifespan

Truck tires represent one of the largest recurring material demands in fleet operations. Retreading programs reuse tire casings while replacing worn tread, allowing tires to safely operate through multiple life cycles.

Why it matters

Retreading significantly reduces raw rubber use and manufacturing energy while lowering fleet operating costs.

Extending tire life reduces waste and material demand at scale.

Section 4 — Maintenance Material Recovery

Reducing Service-Related Waste

Fleet maintenance operations generate packaging materials, filters, metal parts, coolant containers, and service supplies. Structured recovery and recycling programs help reduce landfill waste and improve operational sustainability reporting.

Why it matters

When applied across a national fleet, even small material recovery improvements compound into substantial waste reductions.

Operational sustainability includes what happens in the service bay, not just on the road.

Section 5 — Scope 3 Impact

Addressing Lifecycle Supply Chain Impact

Sustainability in transportation extends beyond direct fuel usage. Material sourcing, component manufacturing, and disposal processes all contribute to the overall environmental footprint.

By supporting recycling, remanufacturing, and material recovery programs, fleet operations can help reduce upstream resource demand and downstream waste impact.

A sustainable fleet considers the entire lifecycle of the vehicle and its components.
