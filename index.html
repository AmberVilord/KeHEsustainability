Idle-to-Impact

Practical Logistics Sustainability KeHE Can Start Next Quarter

KeHE operates a large national distribution network where trucks drive long distances, idle during loading and staging, and handle time-sensitive deliveries.

Even highly optimized fleets still lose fuel through extended engine idling, highway aerodynamic drag, and long-haul routes better suited to rail transport. These inefficiencies increase fuel costs and emissions while limiting the company's ability to scale sustainability initiatives.

KeHE needs a solution that is realistic, implementable quickly, financially responsible, and measurable in environmental impact.

Rather than proposing a single new technology, this proposal focuses on combining several proven operational improvements into one measurable, phased implementation strategy.
