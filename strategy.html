Implementation

Phased Rollout Plan

Idle-to-Impact is designed as a practical phased initiative that improves existing assets already in service while maintaining current delivery workflows and service levels.

Rather than proposing a single new technology, this proposal focuses on combining several proven operational improvements into one measurable, phased implementation strategy.

Phase 1 — Pilot (90 days)

  • Select a representative group of trucks across major routes.
  • Install idle reduction systems or telematics-driven idle control.
  • Install SmartWay-verified aerodynamic trailer upgrades where highway usage is highest.
  • Measure idle hours reduction, fuel consumption change, and cost savings per truck.

Phase 2 — Rail Corridor Identification

Identify long-haul corridors suitable for intermodal rail transport and shift only predictable, high-volume routes where rail maintains service reliability.

Operational Continuity

Local truck delivery continues unchanged at destination hubs to preserve final-mile performance.

Phase 3 — Scale

Expand retrofits fleet-wide where pilot ROI is confirmed and deploy rail integration selectively based on performance and route stability.

DPaaS Pilot Layer (Add-On)

KeHE can run a limited Dynamic Platooning-as-a-Service pilot on selected long-haul interstate corridors where schedule reliability is highest.

  • partner with a third-party platform that matches compatible trucks across carriers
  • use existing adaptive cruise, collision systems, telematics, and fleet tracking data
  • track platoon miles, fuel intensity per mile, safety events, and route timing adherence
  • publish "Efficiency Corridor" reporting tied to emissions and community logistics capacity

Emerging and deployable: software coordination is the primary gap, not truck hardware.
