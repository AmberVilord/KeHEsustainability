Community Impact

Built-In Community Layer

Idle-to-Impact includes a practical reinvestment model: a small portion of operating savings can fund structured logistics support programs that use existing delivery infrastructure.

  • transporting donated shelf-stable food to regional food banks
  • supporting distribution access for smaller regional suppliers
  • coordinating surplus recovery routing within existing deliveries

Because these programs run on the same routes and assets already in operation, they expand community impact without increasing transportation miles.

Selective rail corridor adoption also lowers emissions across entire regions, strengthening KeHE's sustainability footprint in the communities it serves.
